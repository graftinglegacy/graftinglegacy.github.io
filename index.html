Investment Criteria

Seller Criteria


  • Single Owner/Operator within State of Georgia
  • Motivated due to personal reasons such as retirement
  • Willing to facilitate smooth transition of ownership and responsibilities

Business Criteria


  • Strong Reputation
  • At least 3-5 years of consistent profitability
  • Underutilized Technology
  • EBITDA below $1M

Industry Criteria


  • Fragmented but sizeable industry (>$2B)
  • Non-cyclical
  • Consistent Industry Growth

The Process

Grafting Legacy's seller-engagement procedures are as follows:

  • Introductory Communication

    • Intro Call
    • High-level business model overview
    • Discuss Owner's Disposition for Selling the Business

  • High-Level Due Diligence

    • Sign Confidentiality Agreement
    • Review most recent Financial Statements

  • Deep-Dive Due Diligence

    • Discuss Owner's Intentions and Aspirations
    • Sign Letter of Intent
    • Begin Due Diligence Period

  • Close the Deal

    • Finalize Deal Terms Details
    • Sign-off on Deal
    • Transfer Funds to Designated Party

  • Begin
    Management
    Transition!

Grafting Legacy Principals

Nir N. Levy and Ari O. Edlin

The following attributes possessed by Grafting Legacy’s Principals will allow them to be successful:

  • 6 years executing operational improvements with senior leadership in order to increase EBITDA
  • 2 years experience in corporate development, identifying market and business opportunities
  • 2 years of enterprise sales leadership experience
  • Strong support from network of advisors and experienced investors and operators

Nir Levy studied Finance at Emory University. He is a serial entrepreneur with experience working for high growth technology companies in corporate development and vendor management. Nir is a founder of Campus Bubble, a technology company developing apps for Higher Education. He sold the platform to 12 universities before selling the business to Student Bridge. After that, Nir worked for Rainmaker Group, a technology company with revenue management products. Rainmaker Group subsequently sold its LRO product to RealPage for $300 million. Nir then worked for Rubicon Global, “Uber for trash”, where Nir’s team focused on a data and analytics approach to reducing supply chain costs while increasing service coverage to a national scope.

Ari Edlin studied Computer Information Systems within Georgia State University’s Robinson College of Business and is a Certified Scrum Master from his work for Zelis Healthcare. Ari’s unique professional experience is focused around managing enterprise software development projects through Agile in addition to implementing proprietary and vendor-owned technology. As a Senior Analyst, Ari reported directly to senior leadership and was responsible for defining software requirements. Ari was the key leader in launching a data cleansing product that resulted in $1.2 million in yearly recurring revenue. Ari’s family has several small business owners whom have lived in Atlanta for over six generations. He is uniquely positioned to relate to retiring business owners based in the Southeast.
